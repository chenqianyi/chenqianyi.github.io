Discussion of advantages and disadvantages of using fiscal to control inflation

Fiscal policy is a macro intervention that
government uses its power to alter with aggregate demand, that refers to tax (related
with disposable income) or government spending. There are two types of fiscal
policy, one is expansionary fiscal policy, the other is contractionary fiscal
policy. Differences exist between these two policies. Expansionary aims to
increase AD: either lower tax to increase disposable income or increase
government spending. That means people have larger purchasing power, so they
are more likely to buy things, which leads AD increase. Moreover,
contractionary fiscal policy aims to let AD decrease, through tax or government
spending decrease, caused budget surplus.

Fiscal policy has both advantages and
disadvantages.

Fiscal policy can through lower tax and government spending to stimulate the economy.
Since higher consumption or government spending create
job opportunities. In this case, more people being employed means people with higher income level.
As a result, the economy can be recovered, people will live with a better
living standard. Then, because of the government spending are mostly on
infrastructure, education and health care, which are all benefit for all the
people. It is a targeting policy, so the efficiency level can be higher thus
the income level can be higher.

However, there are some drawbacks of fiscal policy. The first one is
the limitation of it. It needs to be applied when the economy not reaching full
capacity, when the economy is near full capacity, the fiscal policy with
limited effect. The second one is it will cost budget deficit for government.
In the long run, government may increase tax or issue national debt to remedy
the deficit, cause long run debt burden to consumer increase.

In addition, fiscal policy has both benefits and
drawbacks, but it is really an effective way to control inflation. Government
should judge and weigh between them.
